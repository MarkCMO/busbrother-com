Film and Production Crew Transportation

Florida is one of the busiest film and production states in the country. From major studio productions in Orlando to commercial shoots in Miami, television series filming in Tampa, and independent films on location across the state, the entertainment industry needs reliable group transportation that can keep up with the demanding pace of a production schedule.

Production crew transportation is not like standard charter bus service. Call times change at the last minute. Shoot days run 12 to 16 hours. Locations shift with little notice. Weather delays push schedules back. And when 30 crew members are waiting for a bus to take them from basecamp to set, every minute of delay costs the production money.

BusBrother understands production logistics. We provide charter bus and minibus service built around the unique demands of film and television production - 24/7 availability, flexible scheduling, last-minute changes, and the reliability that production managers and transportation coordinators require. Our carrier network spans all of Florida, giving us the ability to provide vehicles and drivers wherever your production is shooting.

Types of Production Transportation

Cast Shuttles

Transporting cast members between hotels and set locations requires punctuality and professionalism. Late cast arrivals delay the entire production. BusBrother provides dedicated cast shuttles that operate on your call sheet schedule, picking up talent at their hotels and delivering them to hair and makeup, set, or basecamp with time to spare. Vehicles are clean, comfortable, and climate-controlled so cast members arrive ready to work.

Crew Transport

Moving large crews between basecamp and set, between locations during a multi-location shoot day, or between crew hotels and the production office. Crew transport is often the backbone of production transportation - the service that gets the grip and electric team, camera department, art department, and all other departments to where they need to be on time. BusBrother provides minibuses and motorcoaches sized to your crew count with schedules built around your call times.

Location Moves

When a production moves from one location to another during a shoot day, the entire cast and crew needs to relocate quickly and efficiently. A location move might involve transporting 50 to 100 people from a downtown street to a suburban house 20 miles away - and getting everyone there in time to resume shooting on schedule. BusBrother coordinates multi-vehicle location moves with staggered departures to ensure departments arrive in the right order.

Basecamp Shuttles

When the shooting location is distant from basecamp (where trailers, catering, and crew staging are set up), a continuous shuttle loop between basecamp and set keeps people moving throughout the day. Department heads, producers, directors, and crew members need to move back and forth between set and basecamp regularly, and a dedicated shuttle running on a 15 to 20 minute loop makes this seamless.

Equipment Transport Support

While BusBrother's primary service is passenger transportation, our larger motorcoaches include substantial under-coach luggage bays that can supplement equipment transport for smaller items, personal gear, wardrobe, and production supplies. For departments that need to move cases, monitors, or support equipment alongside personnel, the luggage capacity of a full-size coach can be valuable.

Scouting and Tech Survey Transportation

Before principal photography begins, location scouts, directors, cinematographers, and department heads visit potential filming locations to evaluate them. A minibus provides comfortable transportation for scouting teams visiting multiple locations in a single day, with the flexibility to adjust the route as decisions are made on the fly.

Why Productions Choose Charter Bus

Many productions start by considering passenger vans or individual rideshare rides for crew transport. Here is why charter buses and minibuses are the better choice:

  • ✓Cost efficiency: A single 35-passenger minibus costs less per person than booking 35 individual rideshare rides or renting 7 passenger vans with 7 drivers. The math gets even better with a 56-passenger motorcoach.
  • ✓Keep the crew together: When the entire department rides together, they arrive together. No stragglers, no one getting lost, no one stuck in traffic while the rest of the crew waits on set.
  • ✓On-time arrivals: A professional CDL driver with GPS and knowledge of local roads gets your crew to set on time, every time. That is their one job, and they are good at it.
  • ✓Reduced liability: Having crew members drive personal vehicles or rental vans to and from set creates liability exposure for the production company. Professional charter service with a licensed, insured carrier shifts that liability off the production.
  • ✓Crew rest: After a 14-hour shoot day, the last thing crew members should be doing is driving themselves back to the hotel. A charter bus lets exhausted crew members rest on the ride back, reducing the risk of fatigue-related accidents.
  • ✓Parking reduction: Location filming often has extremely limited parking. A single charter bus replaces 20 to 50 personal vehicles, dramatically reducing the parking footprint at the location and at basecamp.

Flexible Scheduling for Changing Shoot Times

Anyone who has worked in production knows that schedules change constantly. Weather delays push call times back. A scene wraps early and the next location move happens two hours ahead of schedule. An actor's flight is delayed and the entire day's shooting order gets rearranged.

BusBrother builds flexibility into every production transportation booking:

  • Same-day schedule changes: Contact your driver or BusBrother's 24/7 support line to adjust pickup times, routes, or destinations during the production day
  • Add-on runs: Need an extra shuttle run that was not in the original schedule? We can often accommodate additional runs with the same vehicle and driver if they are on standby
  • Extended hours: If the shoot day runs longer than planned, your vehicle can stay in service (subject to driver HOS compliance)
  • Weather day standby: If a shoot is weather-dependent and might be called off, we can arrange standby service where the vehicle is available if needed but not charged at full rate if cancelled with sufficient notice

Frequently Asked Questions for Productions

Can the driver stay on set all day or do they leave and come back?

For production work, we recommend booking the vehicle and driver for the full day (on standby). The driver remains at basecamp or a designated staging area throughout the shoot day, ready to make runs as needed. This eliminates wait times when you need a shuttle and gives you maximum flexibility. Drivers on standby can make multiple runs throughout the day as your schedule requires.

How do you handle multi-week productions?

For productions shooting over multiple weeks, BusBrother provides weekly or multi-week booking packages with consistent vehicle and driver assignments. Your driver gets to know your crew, your locations, and your production's rhythm, which improves efficiency as the shoot progresses. Multi-week bookings typically qualify for discounted daily rates.

What if our shoot location changes the night before?

It happens all the time in production. Contact BusBrother or your assigned driver with the updated location as soon as you know. We will adjust the route and pickup plan. For locations within the same general area, there is typically no price change. For significant distance changes, we will advise if any rate adjustment is needed.

Can you provide transportation for a location scout visiting 8 to 10 locations in one day?

Absolutely. Scouting days are a perfect use case for a minibus with a full-day driver. Your scouting team boards in the morning, visits each potential location on the list, and the driver navigates between them while your team reviews photos, discusses options, and plans the next stop. It is much more efficient and comfortable than cramming into personal vehicles.
