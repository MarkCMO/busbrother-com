Preparing for Your {{airportCode}} to {{cityName}} Shuttle

A smooth {{airportCode}} to {{cityName}} shuttle depends on a few pieces of information exchanged between your group and the carrier before trip day. Book with the following in hand and everything will run cleanly:

  • Flight numbers for every arriving passenger. The carrier uses flight numbers to monitor delays and stage the driver at the correct terminal. If your group is arriving on multiple flights, list every flight number and expected arrival time.
  • Total passenger count with a small buffer. If someone in your group is on the fence about attending, communicate the maximum possible headcount so the correct vehicle is dispatched. A 30-passenger group booked on a 25-passenger minibus is a bad day.
  • Luggage count. Standard motorcoaches and premium coaches have generous underbus luggage compartments, but cruise groups often arrive with 1-2 checked bags plus carry-on per person. If your group's total bag count is unusually high, mention it so the carrier can confirm capacity or dispatch a luggage-trailer if needed.
  • Final destination address in {{cityName}}. Full street address, hotel name, or event venue — not just the city. If you have multiple drop-off points, list them in the order you want them served.
  • Special requirements. ADA wheelchair passengers, service animals, child car seats, oversized instruments or equipment, and any medical considerations. Mention these at booking, not at the terminal.
  • Trip leader phone number. The carrier will contact your designated trip leader on trip day if there is a schedule change, unusual weather, or airport disruption. Choose someone who reliably has their phone with them and who can decide on behalf of the group.
  • Return trip details if applicable. If you are booking round-trip, provide the return date, departure flight number, and pickup address at booking — not the night before you fly home.

On trip day, look for your driver at the baggage claim exit of your arrival terminal. Drivers hold a printed sign with your group name (as provided at booking). If you cannot find your driver within 10 minutes of clearing baggage claim, call the carrier's dispatch phone number (provided on your booking confirmation) — do not leave the terminal expecting the driver to find you. Ground transportation at {{airportCode}} is strictly regulated and drivers cannot circle the arrivals curb waiting for a delayed party.

Once your group is loaded and the driver confirms the headcount matches the manifest, the vehicle departs for {{cityName}} via {{primaryHighway}}. Estimated arrival is your scheduled pickup time plus {{driveMinutes}} minutes. If your final destination is a hotel with a specific check-in time (most Florida hotels are 3-4pm), the driver's dispatch will coordinate with you to prevent an awkward wait in the hotel lobby with your group's luggage piled up.
