Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 25, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, phone) when you visit a website. They allow the site to remember information about your visit, such as your preferences, login state, or browsing activity. BusBrother (operated by WETYR Corporation) uses cookies and similar technologies (local storage, pixels, web beacons) on busbrother.com to operate the site, analyze traffic, and improve our services.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function. They enable core features such as form submissions, secure quote requests, and vendor login on the bidding portal. You cannot opt out of strictly necessary cookies because the site will not work without them.

  • Session identifiers for the booking form
  • CSRF tokens to protect against cross-site request forgery
  • Vendor authentication tokens for the bidding portal

2.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to understand how visitors interact with the site so we can improve content, layout, and load speed. These cookies collect aggregated, anonymized data such as the pages you visit, time on site, and traffic source.

  • Google Analytics - traffic measurement and conversion tracking
  • Netlify Analytics - server-side aggregate metrics

2.3 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies remember your preferences (such as the city you last viewed, or whether you have dismissed a banner) so we can give you a more tailored experience on return visits.

2.4 Advertising and Retargeting Cookies

We may use advertising cookies (Google Ads, Meta Pixel) to show relevant ads on other websites after you visit busbrother.com. These cookies track conversions and help us measure ad effectiveness. You can opt out of personalized advertising via the Network Advertising Initiative or your browser settings.

3. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third-party services we use to operate the site, including:

  • Google - Analytics, Fonts, reCAPTCHA
  • Square - Payment processing for invoices
  • Resend - Transactional email delivery (no client-side cookies)
  • Supabase - Backend database for vendor bids
  • Netlify - Hosting, forms, and edge functions

Each third party has its own privacy and cookie policy that governs its use of your data.

4. How to Control Cookies

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers let you:

  • View cookies stored on your device
  • Delete some or all cookies
  • Block all cookies from being set
  • Block cookies from specific sites
  • Block third-party cookies

Note: blocking strictly necessary cookies may break parts of busbrother.com, including the quote request form and the vendor portal.

5. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal to websites. There is no industry-wide consensus on how DNT should be honored, and busbrother.com currently does not respond to DNT signals. We do honor opt-outs through cookie banners, browser settings, and the Network Advertising Initiative opt-out tool.

6. Cookies and Children

BusBrother is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information or set tracking cookies on children's devices. If you believe a child has submitted information to us, contact info@busbrother.com and we will delete it.

7. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy as our site, technologies, or legal requirements evolve. The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent revision. Material changes will be announced on the homepage or via email if you have an active booking.

8. Contact Us

Questions about cookies or this policy? Reach the BusBrother team at info@busbrother.com. For more on how we handle personal data, see our Privacy Policy.
